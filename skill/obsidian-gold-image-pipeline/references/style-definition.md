# Obsidian Gold Style Definition

Style core version: 1.0.0

## 1. Intent

Obsidian Gold presents a subject as a controlled digital sculpture: stable, restrained, valuable, technological, and premium without decorative excess.

The style is defined by explicit visual properties. The phrase `Obsidian Gold style` alone is never sufficient instruction.

## 2. Mandatory invariants

### Subject and composition

- Exactly one dominant object or one fused metaphor.
- The object is fully visible and isolated.
- No environment, room, landscape, floor plane, pedestal, prop field, or narrative background.
- Generous negative space surrounds the object.
- Centered or slightly offset composition is allowed.
- Default view is front three-quarter or another view that maximizes recognition and silhouette clarity.

### Geometry

- Faceted, low-poly, angular, or parametrically planar construction.
- Clean edges and deliberate sharp folds.
- Simplified, icon-grade silhouette.
- No micro-detail that disappears at 64 px.
- Organic subjects are translated into sculptural planes rather than realistic anatomy or fur detail.

### Obsidian material

- Primary material is matte obsidian-black.
- Surface is smooth and manufactured, not mineral rock.
- No veins, pores, cracks, chips, rough stone, or natural crystal texture.
- No mirror or chrome behavior.
- Broad, dim shape highlights are allowed only to reveal planes.

### Gold material

- Gold is a controlled accent, not the body material.
- Target visible area is 15-25% of the object.
- Preferred placements: rim, trim, band, tip, edge, inset, mask, seam, inner core, or restrained glow.
- Gold is satin by default.
- Small controlled specular highlights are allowed.
- Mirror, chrome, jewelry sparkle, and HDR bloom are forbidden.

### Lighting

- One broad soft key light, slightly elevated and offset from camera.
- Lighting reveals geometry rather than texture.
- Optional restrained warm edge accent may support silhouette or gold placement.
- No visible environment reflection, multiple rim lights, theatrical beams, haze, or strong floor shadow.

### Background and palette

- Final background is pure black `#000000`.
- No gradient, vignette color, smoke, stars, particles, texture, or horizon.
- Palette is limited to obsidian-black, tonal black used for object readability, and gold.
- White may appear only as a tiny unavoidable highlight, never as a material or design color.

### Output character

- The result reads as a digital sculpture or premium icon, not a photograph.
- The silhouette remains recognizable at 64 px.
- The object remains visually restrained and uncluttered.

## 3. Allowed variation

The following may vary when they do not violate mandatory invariants:

- exact facet count and plane topology;
- degree of abstraction;
- centered versus slightly offset placement;
- front, three-quarter, side, or elevated camera angle;
- gold placement within the 15-25% range;
- subtle internal gold glow;
- square or explicitly requested delivery ratio;
- object orientation and pose when recognition features remain intact.

## 4. Critical rejection conditions

Reject the result regardless of score when any of the following is present:

- wrong primary subject or lost semantic meaning;
- multiple competing objects;
- environmental scene or non-black background;
- stone, crystal, marble, lava, or rough mineral texture;
- reflective chrome or mirror-like obsidian;
- gold above 25% of visible object area;
- additional design colors;
- photorealistic product photography;
- illegible silhouette at 64 px;
- cropped object;
- generated text, pseudo-text, watermark, or unintended logo;
- result not available for visual inspection or user-visible delivery.

## 5. Style vocabulary

Preferred descriptive concepts:

```text
single isolated object
fused visual metaphor
faceted geometric construction
low-poly sculptural planes
parametric angular surfaces
clean edges and sharp folds
matte obsidian-black body
controlled satin gold accents
broad soft key light
pure black background
premium digital sculpture
icon-grade silhouette
```

Avoid magical style-name prompting and describe the properties explicitly.
